Best Free Photo Editing Apps in 2026 (Mobile + Desktop)

Updated February 27, 2026 · 12 min read

Professional photo editing no longer requires expensive software or a desktop computer. In 2026, free apps on your phone and computer can handle everything from basic crops to advanced retouching. We tested the top free editors head-to-head so you don't have to.

Best Mobile Photo Editors

Snapseed (iOS & Android)

Google's free photo editor remains the gold standard for mobile editing. It offers 29 tools and filters, non-destructive editing, and RAW file support — all completely free with no ads or in-app purchases.

  • Selective editing — brush adjustments onto specific areas of your photo
  • Healing tool — remove unwanted objects with a tap
  • Curves & white balance — professional-grade color control
  • Expand tool — AI extends your canvas using content-aware fill
  • Stacks — view and undo individual edits non-destructively
Best for: Anyone who wants desktop-level editing on their phone. Completely free, no watermarks, no account required.

VSCO (iOS & Android)

Known for its film-inspired presets, VSCO gives photos a cinematic look that's difficult to replicate elsewhere. The free tier includes 10 presets and full manual editing tools.

  • Film presets — emulate Kodak, Fuji, and Agfa film stocks
  • HSL controls — fine-tune individual color channels
  • Grain & fade — add authentic analog texture
  • Video editing — apply the same presets to video clips
Free tier: 10 presets + all editing tools. VSCO+ ($29.99/year) unlocks 200+ presets and AI features.

Adobe Lightroom Mobile (iOS & Android)

Lightroom's free mobile app is surprisingly powerful. You get the same core editing tools as the desktop version without paying for Creative Cloud.

  • Auto-enhance — AI analyzes and corrects exposure, color, and tone
  • Masking — select subject, sky, or background with one tap
  • Presets — create and import custom presets for consistent looks
  • RAW support — shoot and edit RAW directly in the app
  • Cloud sync — edits sync across all devices (requires free Adobe account)
Free vs Paid: The free tier covers 90% of editing needs. Paid ($9.99/mo) adds healing brush, selective adjustments, and cloud storage.

Best Desktop Photo Editors

Photopea (Browser-Based)

Photopea runs entirely in your browser and supports PSD, XCF, Sketch, XD, and CDR files. It's the closest free alternative to Photoshop, with layers, masks, blend modes, and smart objects.

  • Full PSD support — open and edit Photoshop files with layers intact
  • Pen tool & vectors — create precise selections and vector shapes
  • Layer styles — drop shadows, glows, strokes, and gradients
  • Batch processing — automate repetitive edits with scripts
  • No installation — works in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge
Best for: Photoshop users who need a free alternative. Opens real PSD files. No download or account needed.

GIMP (Windows, Mac, Linux)

The GNU Image Manipulation Program has been the open-source Photoshop alternative since 1996. Version 3.0 (released 2024) brought a modern UI, non-destructive editing, and CMYK support.

  • Full layer support — layer groups, masks, blend modes
  • Extensive plugin library — thousands of free plugins and scripts
  • Customizable UI — single-window or multi-window mode
  • Batch processing — automate with Script-Fu or Python
  • 100% free and open-source — no ads, no tracking, no subscriptions

Canva (Browser-Based)

While primarily a design tool, Canva's photo editor has become surprisingly capable. One-click background removal, AI-powered enhancements, and an intuitive interface make it ideal for quick edits.

  • Background remover — one-click removal (Pro feature, free trial available)
  • Magic Eraser — remove unwanted objects from photos
  • Filters & adjustments — brightness, contrast, saturation, and 20+ filters
  • Direct social export — resize and post to Instagram, Facebook, X in one click

Best Editor for Each Workflow

Quick social media post: Snapseed or Canva. Fast edits, easy export, no learning curve.
Film/aesthetic look: VSCO. Unmatched film emulation presets.
Batch editing 100+ photos: Lightroom (desktop) or GIMP with Script-Fu.
Detailed retouching/compositing: Photopea or GIMP. Full layer support and precision tools.
Product photography: Lightroom for color accuracy, then Photopea for background replacement.
RAW photo development: Lightroom Mobile (free) or desktop. Non-destructive RAW processing.

Photo Editing Tips for Beginners

  1. Start with exposure and white balance. Get the foundation right before adding effects. A properly exposed, color-balanced photo needs fewer adjustments.
  2. Don't over-saturate. Beginners almost always push saturation too far. Bump it 10-15% max. Use vibrance instead for more natural results.
  3. Straighten the horizon. A crooked horizon makes every photo look amateur. Most editors have an auto-straighten feature.
  4. Crop with intent. Use the rule of thirds grid. Cut out dead space. Your subject should dominate the frame.
  5. Edit on a calibrated screen. Your phone in Night Mode or a dim laptop screen will produce inconsistent results. Edit at full brightness on a color-accurate display.
  6. Save your original. Always keep an unedited copy. Export edited versions as new files, never overwrite the original.

FAQ

Is Snapseed really 100% free?

Yes. Snapseed is made by Google and is completely free with no ads, no in-app purchases, and no watermarks. All 29 tools and filters are available at no cost on iOS and Android.

Can Photopea actually replace Photoshop?

For 80-90% of tasks, yes. Photopea supports PSD files, layers, masks, blend modes, and most Photoshop filters. It struggles with very large files (500MB+) and lacks some advanced features like Content-Aware Scale. For most users, it's a genuine free replacement.

What's the best free editor for RAW photos?

Adobe Lightroom Mobile (free tier) is the best free RAW editor. It handles CR2, NEF, ARW, and DNG files with non-destructive editing. On desktop, RawTherapee and Darktable are excellent open-source alternatives.
