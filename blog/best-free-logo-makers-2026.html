Logo Design Tips

  1. Keep it simple. The best logos work at any size — from a favicon to a billboard. Complex logos fail at small sizes.
  2. Use 2-3 colors maximum. Your logo needs to work in full color, single color, and black/white.
  3. Choose readable fonts. If people can't read your logo at a glance, it's not working.
  4. Make it scalable. Use vector formats (SVG) so your logo stays crisp at any size.
  5. Test on different backgrounds. Your logo should work on light, dark, and colored backgrounds.
  6. Avoid trends. Trendy logos look dated in 2 years. Classic designs last decades.

Common Logo Design Mistakes

  • Too many colors. Stick to 2-3. Your printer and brand consistency will thank you.
  • Too much detail. If it doesn't work as a 32x32 favicon, simplify it.
  • Using raster instead of vector. Always create in SVG first, then export to PNG.
  • Copying competitors. Your logo should differentiate, not blend in.
  • Skipping the black/white test. If your logo doesn't work without color, the design is weak.

FAQ

Can I use a free logo for my business legally?

Yes, but check the license. Hatchful and Canva free logos are yours to use commercially. AI generators may require a paid plan for commercial use rights. Always verify before printing business cards.

What file format should my logo be in?

SVG (vector) is the master format — it scales infinitely. Export PNG for web use and PDF for print. Always keep the original SVG file.

Should I hire a designer or use a logo maker?

For startups and side projects, logo makers are perfect. For established businesses seeking a unique brand identity, invest in a professional designer ($500-2,000 for a quality logo).
