1. Photopea — The Photoshop Replacement

Photopea is the most feature-complete free photo editor in existence. It runs entirely in your browser, requires no account or download, and supports PSD, XCF, Sketch, XD, CDR, and every image format you can name. Its interface is nearly identical to Adobe Photoshop, and it supports the same keyboard shortcuts. If you know Photoshop, you already know Photopea.

Best for: Professional-grade editing, PSD file compatibility, layer-based compositing, and users who need a full Photoshop replacement without the $22.99/month subscription.
  • Price: Free (ad-supported). Premium removes ads for $5/month.
  • Account required: No — open the site and start editing immediately
  • Layers: Full support including layer masks, blend modes, adjustment layers, smart objects
  • Selection tools: Magic wand, lasso, pen tool, quick selection, color range, subject selection (AI)
  • Filters: Blur, sharpen, noise, distort, liquify, lens correction, HDR toning
  • Text: Full typography with OpenType features, paragraph styles, text on path
  • Raw support: Yes — opens .CR2, .NEF, .ARW, .DNG raw camera files
  • Export formats: PSD, PNG, JPG, WebP, AVIF, SVG, PDF, GIF, BMP, TIFF
  • File size limit: Limited by your browser's available memory (typically handles files up to ~500MB)

What Photopea Does That Others Don't

Photopea's killer advantage is PSD compatibility. You can open a layered Photoshop file — with masks, adjustment layers, smart objects, blend modes, and effects — and edit it directly. This is invaluable for designers who receive PSD files from clients or collaborators but don't have a Photoshop subscription.

The tool also supports non-destructive editing through adjustment layers. Instead of permanently altering pixel data, you can stack Color Balance, Curves, Levels, Hue/Saturation, and other adjustments as separate layers that can be modified or removed at any time. This professional workflow is rare in free online editors.

Photopea Performance

Because Photopea runs in the browser using WebAssembly, performance depends on your hardware. On a modern laptop with 8GB+ RAM, it handles 50-megapixel images smoothly. Operations like Gaussian Blur, Liquify, and Perspective Warp run at near-native speed. The biggest bottleneck is initial file loading for very large PSDs (100MB+), which can take 10-30 seconds.

2. Pixlr — AI-Powered Quick Edits

Pixlr has reinvented itself as an AI-first photo editor. While it still offers manual editing tools (layers, masks, brushes), its strongest features are now AI-powered: one-click background removal, AI object eraser, AI sky replacement, auto-enhance, and AI-generated backgrounds. It's the fastest path from "raw photo" to "polished result" for users who don't want to learn complex editing tools.

Best for: Quick edits and AI-powered enhancements. Social media content creators, marketers, and casual users who need fast results without a learning curve.
  • Price: Free (with watermark on AI features). Plus plan removes watermark ($7.99/month).
  • Account required: Optional (account unlocks cloud storage and history)
  • Two editors: Pixlr X (quick edits, simple interface) and Pixlr E (advanced, layer-based)
  • AI features: Background removal, object eraser, sky replacement, auto-enhance, AI expand
  • Templates: Thousands of pre-made social media, marketing, and presentation templates
  • Overlays: Light leaks, bokeh, textures, gradients, borders, and sticker libraries
  • Batch editing: Yes — apply edits to multiple photos at once
  • Export formats: PNG, JPG, WebP, PXD (Pixlr native)

3. Fotor — One-Click Enhancements

Fotor is designed for people who want professional-looking results with minimal effort. Its one-click enhancement engine analyzes your photo's exposure, contrast, saturation, and sharpness, then applies optimized adjustments automatically. Think of it as an intelligent auto-correct that actually works well.

Best for: Non-designers who need to quickly improve photos for social media, blogs, or e-commerce listings. Fotor's one-click approach is fastest-to-result for casual photo enhancement.
  • Price: Free (limited features). Pro plan unlocks all tools ($8.99/month).
  • Account required: Optional (account enables cloud saving)
  • One-click enhance: AI analyzes and corrects exposure, color, sharpness, and contrast automatically
  • Effects: 100+ photo filters organized by style (vintage, film, B&W, HDR, pop art)
  • Beauty tools: Blemish removal, skin smoothing, teeth whitening, red-eye fix, face reshape
  • Collage maker: Grid layouts, freestyle layouts, photo stitching
  • Batch processing: Yes — apply the same edits to multiple photos
  • Export formats: PNG, JPG

Fotor's Enhancement Engine

Fotor's auto-enhancement is genuinely impressive for a free tool. It doesn't just boost brightness and saturation uniformly. The AI identifies underexposed shadows, overexposed highlights, color cast issues, and sharpness deficiencies separately, then applies targeted corrections. Results are typically 80-90% as good as a manual Lightroom edit for well-shot photos.

The tool is particularly strong for product photography. E-commerce sellers can upload product shots, apply one-click enhancement, use the background remover for white-background isolation, and export web-ready images in under 60 seconds per photo.

4. Canva Photo Editor — Design-First Editing

Canva is primarily a graphic design platform, but its built-in photo editor has become surprisingly powerful. It won't replace Photopea for complex retouching, but for editing photos as part of a design workflow — social media posts, presentations, marketing materials — Canva's photo editing tools are seamlessly integrated and incredibly convenient.

Best for: Editing photos within a design context. If you're creating social media graphics, presentations, or marketing materials, Canva lets you edit and design in the same tool without exporting and re-importing.
  • Price: Free (generous free tier). Pro plan $12.99/month (more AI credits, premium assets).
  • Account required: Yes (free account)
  • Photo adjustments: Brightness, contrast, saturation, tint, blur, sharpen, vignette, warmth
  • AI features: Magic Eraser (object removal), Background Remover, Magic Expand, Magic Edit (text-to-edit)
  • Filters: 20+ preset filters with intensity sliders
  • Frames & mockups: Drop photos into device frames, product mockups, and artistic borders
  • Integration: Direct access to Pexels, Pixabay stock photos while editing
  • Export formats: PNG, JPG, PDF, SVG, MP4, GIF

Canva's Magic Studio Tools

Canva's AI suite (called Magic Studio) is the most accessible implementation of generative AI in any photo editor. The standout features:

  • Magic Eraser: Brush over any object in a photo and it's removed with AI-generated fill. Works for people, objects, text, watermarks, and blemishes. Free tier gets limited uses per month.
  • Background Remover: One-click background removal with edge refinement. Accuracy is on par with remove.bg for most subjects.
  • Magic Expand: Extend the edges of a photo beyond its original boundaries using AI-generated content. Useful for fitting a portrait-orientation photo into a landscape frame.
  • Magic Edit: Select an area and describe what you want in text. "Replace this blue sky with a sunset" or "add a coffee cup to the table." The AI generates the edit within the selection.

Best for: E-commerce product photos, headshots, social media cutouts, and any workflow where you need to isolate a subject from its background. remove.bg's edge quality is best-in-class.

6. cleanup.pictures — Object and Blemish Removal

cleanup.pictures is a specialized tool for removing unwanted objects, people, text, watermarks, and blemishes from photos. Brush over anything you want gone, and the AI fills the area with contextually appropriate content. It's like Photoshop's Content-Aware Fill, but in a free browser tool with zero learning curve.

Best for: Removing unwanted elements from otherwise good photos. Tourist photobombs, power lines, trash cans, skin blemishes, text overlays, date stamps — anything you want gone.
  • Price: Free (standard resolution). Pro plan for HD ($5/month).
  • Account required: No
  • How it works: Brush over the unwanted area, AI generates replacement content matching the surrounding context
  • Best at: Removing objects against textured backgrounds (grass, sky, walls, floors)
  • Struggles with: Complex scenes where the removed object occludes important detail
  • Processing: Real-time — results appear as you paint
  • Export: PNG, JPG

Use Cases

  • Real estate photography: Remove cars from driveways, trash bins from sidewalks, power lines from sky
  • Portrait retouching: Remove skin blemishes, scars, stray hairs without the complexity of Photoshop's healing brush
  • Product photography: Clean up reflections, remove labels, erase dust spots on product surfaces
  • Travel photography: Remove tourists, signs, and modern objects from scenic landscape shots
  • Social media: Remove watermarks from your own photos (never from others' copyrighted images)

The tool works best when the area you're removing is surrounded by consistent texture. Removing a person standing on a grassy field produces clean results because the AI easily generates matching grass. Removing a person standing in front of a complex architectural detail is harder because the AI has to reconstruct structure, not just texture.

8. Common Editing Workflows (Step-by-Step)

Workflow 1: Product Photo for E-Commerce

Step 1: Upload your product photo to remove.bg and download the transparent PNG (subject isolated from background).
Step 2: Open the transparent PNG in Photopea. Create a white background layer beneath the subject.
Step 3: Use Photopea's Levels adjustment (Ctrl+L) to brighten the product and increase contrast. Adjust white balance if the product's color looks off.
Step 4: Add a subtle drop shadow (Layer > Layer Style > Drop Shadow) to give the product dimensionality on the white background.
Step 5: Export as JPG at 80% quality for web listings or PNG for transparency-preserving platforms.

Workflow 2: Social Media Post

Step 1: Open Canva and select the correct dimensions for your platform (1080x1080 for Instagram, 1200x675 for Twitter/X).
Step 2: Upload your photo and use Canva's auto-enhance to correct exposure and color. Fine-tune with manual sliders if needed.
Step 3: Add text overlays with brand-consistent fonts and colors. Use Canva's alignment guides to center text.
Step 4: Apply a consistent filter across all photos in a series for brand cohesion.
Step 5: Download as PNG for maximum quality, or JPG if file size matters for upload speed.

Workflow 3: Portrait Retouching

Step 1: Open the portrait in Photopea. Duplicate the background layer (Ctrl+J) so you're working non-destructively.
Step 2: Use the Spot Healing Brush (J) to remove blemishes, acne, and stray hairs. Click each blemish once.
Step 3: Apply Gaussian Blur to a duplicate of the retouched layer (Filter > Blur > Gaussian Blur, 5-10px). Add a layer mask and paint the blur only onto skin areas, avoiding eyes, lips, hair, and eyebrows. This smooths skin while keeping important details sharp.
Step 4: Add a Curves adjustment layer. Create a slight S-curve to boost contrast in the midtones.
Step 5: Use cleanup.pictures to remove any remaining distracting elements in the background.

9. Pro Tips for Better Online Editing

Tip 1: Start with the best photo possible. No editor can fix a blurry, poorly lit, or badly composed photo. Shoot with good lighting, steady hands, and intentional framing. Editing should enhance a good photo, not rescue a bad one.
Tip 2: Edit on a calibrated monitor. Your screen's brightness and color settings affect how you perceive edits. A photo that looks perfect on your bright phone screen may look dark and desaturated on someone else's laptop. Edit on a monitor with consistent, accurate colors, or at least turn off Night Shift/blue light filters while editing.
Tip 3: Don't over-edit. The most common beginner mistake is cranking saturation, contrast, and sharpness too high. Subtle adjustments almost always look better than dramatic ones. If your edit looks "edited," you've probably gone too far.
Tip 4: Use keyboard shortcuts. In Photopea: B (brush), V (move), M (marquee), L (lasso), W (magic wand), J (healing brush), Ctrl+Z (undo), Ctrl+Shift+Z (redo). These are the same as Photoshop shortcuts.
Tip 5: Save your work frequently. Browser-based editors can crash if your tab runs out of memory. In Photopea, save as PSD regularly to preserve your layers. Canva auto-saves to the cloud.
Tip 6: Learn non-destructive editing. Use adjustment layers instead of directly modifying pixels. This lets you tweak or remove any adjustment at any time without starting over. Photopea supports full non-destructive workflows with adjustment layers, layer masks, and smart objects.

11. Privacy and Data Handling

When you upload photos to online editors, your images are temporarily stored on their servers for processing. Here's what you should know about each tool's data practices:

  • Photopea: All processing happens in your browser. Images never leave your computer. This is the most private option.
  • Pixlr: Images uploaded for AI processing are temporarily stored and automatically deleted after processing.
  • Canva: Images stored in your account cloud storage. Deleted when you remove them from your account.
  • remove.bg: Images processed on their servers and automatically deleted after 1 hour.
  • cleanup.pictures: Images processed server-side and deleted after processing.
  • Fotor: Images uploaded for processing and deleted after your session ends.

For sensitive or confidential images (medical, legal, private), use Photopea — it's the only tool on this list that processes everything client-side without uploading your data to any server.

FAQ

Is Photopea really as good as Photoshop?

For 90% of tasks, yes. Photopea supports layers, masks, blend modes, adjustment layers, Smart Objects, PSD files, and most Photoshop filters. The main gaps are: no 3D tools, limited video timeline editing, no neural filters, and slightly slower performance on very large files. For photo editing, retouching, compositing, and design work, Photopea is a genuine Photoshop replacement that runs in your browser for free.

Which free online editor is best for beginners?

Canva for design-focused editing (social media posts, presentations) or Fotor for pure photo enhancement. Both have simple interfaces with one-click improvements. Avoid Photopea if you're a complete beginner — its professional interface can be overwhelming. Start with Canva or Fotor, then graduate to Photopea when you need more control.

Can I edit RAW photos online for free?

Yes. Photopea opens RAW files from every major camera manufacturer including Canon (.CR2, .CR3), Nikon (.NEF), Sony (.ARW), and Adobe (.DNG). It provides basic RAW development controls (exposure, white balance, contrast) similar to Adobe Camera Raw. For advanced RAW processing with lens corrections and noise reduction, you'll still want desktop software like RawTherapee (free) or Lightroom.

Do these editors add watermarks to my exported photos?

Photopea: No watermark (ever). Fotor: No watermark on basic features. Canva: No watermark on free content. Pixlr: Watermark on some AI-powered features in the free plan. remove.bg: No watermark but low-resolution output on free tier. cleanup.pictures: No watermark on standard resolution.

Are my uploaded photos safe and private?

Photopea is the safest option — all processing happens in your browser and images never leave your device. All other tools upload images to their servers for processing. Images are typically deleted within 1 hour of processing. For confidential or sensitive photos, use Photopea exclusively.
