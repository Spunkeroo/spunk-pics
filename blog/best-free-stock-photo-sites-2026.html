1. Understanding Stock Photo Licenses

Before downloading anything, you need to understand the license. "Free" doesn't always mean "use however you want." The two licenses you'll encounter most on free stock sites are:

CC0 (Creative Commons Zero): The most permissive license. No attribution required. Use for any purpose including commercial. Modify freely. No restrictions whatsoever. This is effectively "public domain."
Custom permissive license (Unsplash-style): Free for commercial and personal use. No attribution required. Cannot be sold as-is without modification (no reselling as stock photos). Cannot be used to create a competing stock photo service.

Both licenses let you use photos in websites, social media, marketing materials, products, and print without crediting the photographer. The only real restriction is that you can't repackage and sell the photos themselves as stock photography.

2. Unsplash

Unsplash is the most recognized free stock photo platform in the world. With over 4 million high-resolution photos from a global community of photographers, it's the first stop for most creators looking for professional-quality imagery.

Best for: High-quality editorial and lifestyle photography. Unsplash's curatorial standards are higher than most competitors, resulting in consistently professional results.
  • Catalog size: 4+ million photos
  • License: Unsplash License (free commercial use, no attribution required)
  • Resolution: High-res originals (typically 4000-6000px)
  • API: Yes — free tier allows 50 requests/hour
  • Categories: Nature, business, technology, food, travel, architecture, and more
  • Strengths: Consistent quality, excellent search, editorial collections curated by topic

Unsplash's biggest limitation is that its most popular photos are used everywhere. Search for "office meeting" and you'll find the same images on thousands of blogs. Dig deeper with specific, unusual search terms to find less-used photos.

3. Pexels

Pexels combines free stock photos and free stock videos on one platform. Its catalog is massive, its search is fast, and it offers curated collections that make finding thematically consistent sets of images easy.

Best for: Projects that need both photos and videos. Pexels is the only site on this list that offers a large, high-quality video library alongside its photo collection.
  • Catalog size: 3+ million photos, 40,000+ videos
  • License: Pexels License (free commercial use, no attribution required)
  • Resolution: High-res originals with multiple download size options
  • API: Yes — free with generous rate limits
  • Categories: All major categories plus trending topics and seasonal collections
  • Strengths: Photo + video library, fast search, Canva integration

Pexels also integrates directly with Canva, WordPress, and other design tools, letting you search and insert Pexels images without leaving your editor.

4. Pixabay

Pixabay is the largest free media library by total asset count. Beyond photos, it hosts illustrations, vectors, videos, music, and sound effects — all under a permissive license. It's a one-stop shop for free creative assets.

Best for: Creators who need more than just photos. Pixabay's illustration, vector, and audio libraries make it uniquely versatile for multimedia projects.
  • Catalog size: 4+ million assets (photos, illustrations, vectors, videos, music, SFX)
  • License: Pixabay Content License (free commercial use, no attribution required)
  • Resolution: Multiple sizes including high-res originals
  • API: Yes — free tier with 5,000 requests/day
  • Categories: Photos, illustrations, vectors, videos, music, sound effects
  • Strengths: Largest catalog, multimedia assets, generous API

Quality on Pixabay is more variable than Unsplash. The massive catalog includes everything from professional photography to amateur snapshots. Use the "Editor's Choice" filter to find curated, high-quality selections.

5. Burst by Shopify

Burst is Shopify's free stock photo platform, specifically designed for entrepreneurs and e-commerce businesses. The photo collection focuses on product photography, lifestyle images, and business-oriented themes that work well for online stores and marketing materials.

Best for: E-commerce stores, product marketing, Shopify merchants, and business-oriented content. Burst's collection is curated specifically for commercial use cases.
  • Catalog size: Thousands of photos (smaller but highly focused)
  • License: CC0 (Creative Commons Zero) — completely unrestricted
  • Resolution: High-res originals
  • API: No public API
  • Categories: E-commerce, product, lifestyle, business, seasonal, trending niches
  • Strengths: E-commerce focus, CC0 license, business idea bundles

Burst groups photos into "business idea" bundles like "coffee shop," "yoga studio," and "pet supplies," giving you a ready-made photo set for a niche business rather than individual disconnected images.

6. StockSnap

StockSnap adds hundreds of new photos weekly, all under CC0. Its clean interface, trending tracker, and view/download counts help you discover popular images and gauge what's overused. The catalog leans toward modern lifestyle and business photography.

Best for: Finding fresh, recently uploaded photos. StockSnap's trending algorithm and frequent additions mean you're more likely to find images that haven't been used on millions of other sites.
  • Catalog size: Hundreds of thousands of photos
  • License: CC0 (Creative Commons Zero)
  • Resolution: High-res originals
  • API: No public API
  • Categories: Lifestyle, business, nature, technology, food, travel
  • Strengths: Fresh weekly uploads, download tracking, trending algorithm

7. Kaboompics

Kaboompics is a curated collection by photographer Karolina Grabowska. The photos have a distinctive, consistent aesthetic — soft lighting, modern styling, and cohesive color palettes. Each photo comes with a complementary color palette extracted from the image.

Best for: Design-conscious projects where aesthetic consistency matters. Fashion, interior design, food, and lifestyle blogs benefit most from Kaboompics' curated style.
  • Catalog size: 25,000+ photos
  • License: Kaboompics License (free commercial use, no attribution required, no redistribution)
  • Resolution: High-res originals
  • API: No public API
  • Categories: Lifestyle, interior, fashion, food, technology, nature
  • Strengths: Consistent aesthetic, color palettes included, curated quality

The included color palettes are a unique feature. Each photo page shows the dominant colors extracted from the image, making it easy to match your design's color scheme to the photo you're using.

9. Tips for Finding Unique Photos

The biggest problem with free stock photos is overuse. The same "woman smiling at laptop" image appears on thousands of websites. Here's how to find images nobody else is using:

Tip 1: Search with specific, unusual terms. Instead of "business meeting," try "coworking space morning coffee" or "startup whiteboard brainstorm." Longer, more specific queries surface less-used images.
Tip 2: Browse by newest first. Most people use the default "relevant" sort, which surfaces the same popular images. Switch to "newest" to find recently uploaded photos that haven't been downloaded thousands of times yet.
Tip 3: Use multiple sites. Don't rely on just Unsplash. Check Pexels, StockSnap, and Kaboompics for the same search term. Each platform attracts different photographers with different styles.
Tip 4: Crop and edit aggressively. Even an overused photo looks unique when you crop to a detail, apply a duotone filter, or overlay it with text and graphics. The photo is a starting point, not the final product.
Tip 5: Search for photographers, not photos. When you find an image you love, click the photographer's profile. Their other work likely shares the same style and quality, giving you a consistent set of images from a single source.

FAQ

Do I really not need to credit the photographer?

Correct. All six sites listed here allow commercial and personal use without attribution. However, crediting photographers is still a kind gesture that supports the community. If you can include a credit line (especially on blogs or social media), it's appreciated even though it's not legally required.

Can I use free stock photos for client work?

Yes. Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Burst, StockSnap, and Kaboompics all allow commercial use, which includes client projects, marketing campaigns, website designs, and printed materials. The one restriction across all platforms is that you cannot resell the unmodified photos as stock photography.

Are free stock photos safe from copyright claims?

Generally yes, but exercise caution. Reputable platforms verify that uploaders own the rights to their photos, but mistakes happen. For high-stakes commercial use (billboards, national campaigns), consider verifying the image's origin with a reverse image search. Also note that photos containing recognizable people may require separate model releases for commercial advertising use, even if the photo license itself is permissive.
